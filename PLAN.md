# Los Angeles County Roofing Lead Gen Plan

This file is the working plan for expanding the Burbank Roofing site into a Los Angeles County city-domain lead generation network.

## Goal

Build one core roofing website system that can be cloned across Los Angeles County city-focused domains, rank locally for roofing searches, capture and track leads by city, and route those leads to either:

- in-house sales
- exclusive city contractors
- overflow buyers when a city is not sold exclusively

## Core Business Model

- Use the current Burbank Roofing website as the base template.
- Launch city-focused versions on exact-match or close-match domains where practical.
- Generate roofing leads in each city through local SEO.
- Operate some cities directly under your license structure and qualifying contractor relationships.
- Sell the remaining leads either:
  - per lead
  - or as an exclusive monthly city package
- Compare both models by city using close rate, call volume, margin, and contractor retention.

## Operating Assumptions

- Primary market: incorporated cities in Los Angeles County.
- Each city site should share the same technical foundation, but not look mechanically duplicated.
- Every domain needs lead attribution for calls, forms, SMS, and email.
- All inbound leads should go to you first through a master dashboard before redistribution.

## SEO Rollout Plan

### Site Structure

- Keep one shared codebase and one city configuration layer.
- Replace city-specific content through variables, partials, and local assets.
- Standardize these local SEO fields per city:
  - city name
  - page titles
  - meta descriptions
  - H1/H2 copy
  - service area sections
  - FAQ variants
  - local trust copy
  - schema markup
  - phone number
  - tracking IDs

### Content Strategy

- Keep core service pages consistent so maintenance stays simple.
- Create unique homepage intros, testimonials, FAQs, inspection offers, and neighborhood references per city.
- Add city-relevant references such as:
  - roofing style common in the area
  - climate exposure and heat patterns
  - wind, sun, and maintenance concerns
  - local architecture tone
  - nearby neighborhood/service-area mentions
- Build unique supporting pages by city:
  - roof repair in city
  - roof replacement in city
  - flat roofing in city
  - commercial roofing in city
  - emergency roof leak service in city

### Technical SEO Requirements

- Unique title tags and meta descriptions for every city domain.
- Unique local business schema and service schema.
- XML sitemap per domain.
- Google Search Console and GA4 property per domain or per site group.
- Canonical tags must point to the current domain, not the source template.
- Distinct GBP strategy only where there is a legitimate, compliant operating basis.
- Track rankings, indexed pages, calls, form fills, and close rate by city.

## Lead Capture and Attribution

Every lead must be attributable to city, source, and buyer destination.

### Required Attribution Fields

- domain
- city
- landing page
- campaign source
- campaign medium
- campaign name
- keyword where available
- call tracking number used
- form source page
- timestamp
- lead status
- assigned rep or contractor
- disposition outcome

### Call Tracking Recommendations

- Use dynamic phone numbers by domain and optionally by channel.
- Minimum setup: one tracked primary number per city domain.
- Better setup: number pools for paid traffic plus one static tracked local number for organic.
- Record calls where legally permitted and announce recording when required.
- Push call events into the lead dashboard automatically.

Recommended platforms to evaluate:

- CallRail
- WhatConverts
- Twilio with custom routing

### Form Tracking Recommendations

- Every form submits to a central lead intake endpoint.
- Hidden fields should capture domain, city, URL, UTMs, gclid, msclkid, and referring page.
- Trigger instant notification to you by email and SMS.
- Add lead deduplication so repeat inquiries do not get resold accidentally.

## Public Data Enrichment API Requirements

The main enrichment priority is address-to-owner lookup so you can verify who owns the property tied to the incoming roofing lead.

### Primary Enrichment Goal

- take the submitted property address
- standardize and validate the address
- retrieve public property and ownership records where available
- attach the likely property owner name to the lead record
- show confidence and source of the enrichment result

### Secondary Enrichment Goals

- occupancy indicator where available
- parcel or APN number
- assessed value range
- property type
- last sale date
- mailing address when different from service address
- basic entity match if title is held in an LLC or trust

### Important Scope Boundary

- Prioritize official property-record and assessor data tied to the submitted address.
- Do not build the workflow around invasive people-search enrichment from just an email address or phone number.
- If you enrich contact information at all, keep it limited, documented, and tied to a legitimate business workflow and applicable law.

### Recommended Lookup Order

1. Address standardization and validation.
2. Parcel match and property record lookup.
3. Owner name extraction.
4. Mailing-address comparison.
5. Optional business/entity match if ownership is not an individual.

### Suggested API Sources To Evaluate

Address and property data sources:

- ATTOM
- Estated
- Regrid
- DataTree
- county assessor or recorder datasets where API access exists

Support services:

- USPS or commercial address validation
- Google Maps Geocoding for address normalization support

Selection criteria:

- Los Angeles County coverage quality
- owner-name coverage
- parcel/APN coverage
- refresh frequency
- commercial usage rights
- per-request cost
- API reliability

### Enrichment Workflow

1. Lead comes in from form, call intake, or manual entry.
2. Normalize the submitted address.
3. Query property data provider.
4. Store the returned public-record snapshot with timestamp and provider name.
5. Write these fields into the lead dashboard.
6. Mark whether owner match is exact, partial, entity-held, or not found.
7. Route the enriched lead to you first before downstream assignment.

### Lead Record Fields To Store

- submitted name
- submitted email
- submitted phone
- submitted property address
- normalized address
- parcel or APN
- owner name from public records
- mailing owner name if different
- mailing address
- property type
- occupancy hint
- last sale date
- assessed value
- enrichment provider
- enrichment timestamp
- match confidence
- manual review flag

### Matching Logic

- If the submitted last name matches the public owner last name, flag as likely owner-occupied lead.
- If mailing address differs from property address, flag as absentee owner.
- If owner is an LLC, trust, or corporate entity, flag for manual review.
- If no owner is found, keep the lead active but mark enrichment as unresolved.

### Dashboard Requirements For Enrichment

- show owner name next to the lead immediately after lookup
- show whether the address is owner-occupied or absentee when available
- show source provider and confidence
- allow manual override if the returned owner is wrong or outdated
- keep raw API response or selected source fields for audit history

### Practical MVP Approach

- Start with one property data provider for address-to-owner lookup.
- Run enrichment only after a lead is submitted.
- Store only the fields needed for routing, qualification, and reporting.
- Add manual review for LLC, trust, and multi-owner edge cases.

### Future Automation Options

- auto-tag absentee-owner leads for investor-style follow-up
- prioritize owner-occupied residential leads for retail roofing sales
- score commercial or entity-owned properties separately
- segment by property value or property type

### Compliance Notes For Enrichment

- Use only providers and datasets licensed for your commercial use case.
- Treat returned owner data as sensitive business data even if derived from public records.
- Keep audit logs of provider, timestamp, and the fields stored.
- Review vendor terms, California privacy obligations, and any applicable notice requirements.

## Lead Dashboard Requirements

This is the operating hub for all incoming leads across every city domain.

### Dashboard Purpose

- Receive every inbound lead first.
- Review and qualify the lead.
- Assign the lead to:
  - your internal sales rep
  - the exclusive contractor for that city
  - backup buyer or overflow queue
- Log the delivery method and response status.

### Core Dashboard Features

- unified inbox for calls, forms, and SMS inquiries
- filter by city, source, service type, and status
- lead detail view with notes and history
- assignment workflow with one-click routing
- contractor ownership rules by city
- internal rep routing rules
- duplicate detection
- disposition tracking
- revenue tracking by lead and by city
- export to CSV

### Required Status Pipeline

- new
- contacted
- qualified
- assigned
- sold
- won
- lost
- duplicate
- spam

### Routing Rules

- Default: all leads route to you first.
- Then you manually or automatically assign based on city ownership.
- If a city has an exclusive contractor, route by SMS and email to that contractor.
- If city is in-house, route to the internal sales rep.
- If exclusive buyer does not respond within the SLA window, escalate to backup routing.

### SMS and Email Distribution Requirements

- SMS message should include:
  - lead name
  - phone
  - city
  - service requested
  - urgency
  - callback link
- Email should include:
  - full lead details
  - landing page
  - source attribution
  - notes
  - link to dashboard record

### Suggested Tech Stack For Dashboard

Simple first version:

- forms and calls into a Google Sheet or Airtable
- Zapier or Make for routing
- Twilio for SMS
- SendGrid or Postmark for email

Scalable version:

- custom web app
- PostgreSQL database
- Twilio for SMS and call events
- SendGrid/Postmark for email
- simple admin UI for assignment and reporting

Best practical path:

- Start with Airtable plus Twilio plus Zapier for speed.
- Move to a custom dashboard once volume justifies it.

## Revenue Model Decision Framework

Use these rules to decide whether a city should be sold per lead or as an exclusive monthly territory.

### Per-Lead Works Best When

- volume is inconsistent
- contractor quality is uncertain
- multiple buyers are available
- you want price discovery first

### Exclusive Monthly Works Best When

- city produces steady volume
- contractor closes well and pays reliably
- replacement value is high
- you want predictable recurring revenue

### Metrics To Watch

- leads per city per month
- contact rate
- appointment rate
- close rate
- cost per lead
- average revenue per lead
- churn rate per contractor
- payout lag

## Compliance and Licensing Notes

- Keep legal review around advertising, lead resale, contractor relationships, and licensing structure.
- Document which cities are operated directly versus referred.
- Make sure all contractor relationships and lead resale terms are covered by written agreements.
- Review TCPA and state rules for SMS and call handling.
- Review call recording consent requirements for California.
