SPF Foam Roofing

Seamless Spray Polyurethane Foam Roof Systems

Spray Foam Roofing Installation & Restoration

SPF (Spray Polyurethane Foam) roofing is one of the most energy-efficient and versatile roofing systems available. Applied as a liquid that expands into a solid foam, SPF creates a seamless, monolithic barrier that insulates and waterproofs in one application. Burbank Roofing provides professional SPF installation and maintenance.

What is SPF Roofing?

Spray Polyurethane Foam is a two-component system that, when combined, creates an expanding foam that adheres to virtually any substrate. Once cured, the foam is covered with a protective elastomeric coating to protect it from UV rays. The result is a seamless, self-flashing, waterproof roof with superior insulation properties.

Benefits of SPF Foam Roofing

  • Superior Insulation - SPF has the highest R-value per inch of any roofing insulation (R-6.5 per inch), dramatically reducing energy costs.
  • Seamless Application - No seams means no leak points. The entire roof becomes one continuous membrane.
  • Self-Flashing - SPF conforms to and seals around penetrations, skylights, vents, and edges without additional flashing materials.
  • Lightweight - Can often be applied over existing roofing, eliminating costly tear-off and disposal.
  • Renewable - SPF roofs can be recoated indefinitely, potentially lasting 50+ years with proper maintenance.
  • Energy Efficient - Reflective coatings combined with insulation can reduce cooling costs by 30% or more.
  • Strengthens Structure - The rigid foam adds structural integrity to the roof deck.
  • Quick Installation - Minimal disruption to building occupants.

How SPF Roofing is Installed

  1. Preparation - The existing roof is cleaned and any necessary repairs are made.
  2. Foam Application - SPF is spray-applied in multiple passes to achieve the desired thickness (typically 1-2 inches).
  3. Coating Application - An elastomeric protective coating is applied to protect the foam from UV damage.
  4. Final Inspection - The completed roof is inspected for uniform coverage and proper thickness.

SPF Protective Coatings

  • Silicone Coatings - Best for areas with ponding water; excellent UV resistance.
  • Acrylic Coatings - Cost-effective option for roofs with proper drainage.
  • Polyurethane Coatings - Maximum durability for high-traffic roofs.

Ideal Applications for SPF

  • Commercial buildings seeking energy efficiency
  • Buildings with multiple penetrations (HVAC, pipes, vents)
  • Roofs with irregular shapes or complex geometry
  • Re-roofing projects where tear-off is impractical
  • Cold storage and refrigerated facilities
  • Metal building roof restoration
  • Historic buildings requiring lightweight solutions

SPF Roof Maintenance

SPF roofs require regular maintenance to maximize their lifespan:

  • Annual inspections to check coating condition
  • Prompt repair of any coating damage or punctures
  • Recoating every 10-15 years to maintain protection
  • Keep roof clear of debris and standing water
