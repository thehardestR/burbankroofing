Roof Maintenance

Extend Your Roof's Life with Preventive Care

Professional Roof Maintenance Services

A well-maintained roof can last years longer than a neglected one. Regular maintenance catches small problems before they become expensive repairs and helps you get the maximum life from your roofing investment. Burbank Roofing offers comprehensive maintenance services for all roof types.

Our Maintenance Services Include

  • Comprehensive Inspections - Thorough examination of all roofing components, flashing, and penetrations.
  • Debris Removal - Clear leaves, branches, and debris that trap moisture and cause deterioration.
  • Gutter Cleaning - Clean gutters and downspouts to ensure proper drainage.
  • Minor Repairs - Fix small issues like loose shingles, cracked sealant, or minor flashing problems.
  • Moss & Algae Treatment - Remove and treat organic growth that damages roofing materials.
  • Caulking & Sealing - Reseal around vents, pipes, and other penetrations.
  • Ventilation Check - Ensure vents are clear and functioning properly.
  • Documentation - Detailed reports with photos to track your roof's condition over time.

Benefits of Regular Roof Maintenance

  • Extended Roof Life - A maintained roof can last 25-50% longer than a neglected one.
  • Cost Savings - Small repairs cost a fraction of major repairs or premature replacement.
  • Prevent Leaks - Catch and fix problems before water enters your home.
  • Protect Your Warranty - Many warranties require regular maintenance to remain valid.
  • Energy Efficiency - A well-maintained roof provides better insulation and ventilation.
  • Peace of Mind - Know your roof is ready for whatever weather comes.
  • Better Resale Value - Documentation of maintenance is attractive to home buyers.

Maintenance by Roof Type

  • Asphalt Shingles - Check for granule loss, curling, cracking; clean algae and moss.
  • Tile Roofs - Inspect for cracked or broken tiles; check underlayment condition.
  • Metal Roofs - Check fasteners, seams, and coating condition; touch up scratches.
  • Flat Roofs - Clear drains; check for ponding, membrane damage, and seam integrity.
  • Wood Shakes - Treat for moss/rot; replace split or missing shakes.

Recommended Maintenance Schedule

  • Twice Yearly - Spring and fall inspections are ideal for most roofs.
  • After Major Storms - Check for damage after high winds, hail, or heavy rain.
  • When You Notice Problems - Don't wait for your scheduled inspection if you see issues.

DIY vs. Professional Maintenance

Some maintenance tasks homeowners can do:

  • Visual inspection from the ground
  • Keep gutters clear of debris
  • Trim overhanging tree branches
  • Check attic for signs of leaks

Tasks that require professionals:

  • Walking on the roof (safety and damage concerns)
  • Identifying hidden problems
  • Any repairs, no matter how small
  • Working around electrical, HVAC, or plumbing penetrations
