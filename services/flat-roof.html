Flat Roofing

Expert Low-Slope Roofing Solutions

Professional Flat Roof Installation & Repair

Flat and low-slope roofs require specialized knowledge and materials to ensure proper drainage and long-lasting waterproof protection. At Burbank Roofing, we have extensive experience with all types of flat roofing systems for both residential and commercial properties.

Flat Roofing Systems We Install

  • Modified Bitumen - Multi-layer asphalt system with excellent durability and flexibility. Ideal for California's temperature variations.
  • Built-Up Roofing (BUR) - Traditional "tar and gravel" roofing with multiple layers for superior protection.
  • TPO (Thermoplastic Polyolefin) - Energy-efficient single-ply membrane with excellent UV resistance.
  • EPDM Rubber Roofing - Durable synthetic rubber membrane known for its longevity.
  • PVC Roofing - Chemical-resistant membrane ideal for restaurants and facilities with grease exhaust.
  • SPF Foam Roofing - Spray-applied seamless system with superior insulation properties.

Our Flat Roofing Services

  • New Flat Roof Installation - Complete installation with proper drainage design.
  • Flat Roof Replacement - Remove failed roofing and install new system.
  • Flat Roof Repairs - Fix leaks, patch damaged areas, and restore waterproofing.
  • Roof Coatings - Apply reflective coatings to extend roof life and improve energy efficiency.
  • Drainage Solutions - Install or improve drains, scuppers, and gutters.
  • Preventive Maintenance - Regular inspections and maintenance programs.

Benefits of Properly Installed Flat Roofing

  • Usable Space - Flat roofs can accommodate HVAC equipment, solar panels, or rooftop decks.
  • Cost-Effective - Generally less expensive than pitched roofing systems.
  • Easy Access - Simple to inspect and maintain.
  • Energy Efficient - Reflective membranes can significantly reduce cooling costs.
  • Quick Installation - Faster to install than many pitched roof systems.

Common Flat Roof Problems We Fix

  • Ponding Water - Water that doesn't drain within 48 hours can cause membrane deterioration.
  • Membrane Damage - Cracks, blisters, punctures, and seam failures.
  • Flashing Failures - Leaks around edges, penetrations, and equipment.
  • Drainage Issues - Clogged drains, improper slope, or inadequate drainage capacity.
  • UV Damage - Sun exposure breaking down roofing materials.
  • Thermal Movement - Expansion and contraction causing seam and flashing problems.

Why Flat Roofs Need Specialized Contractors

Flat roofing is fundamentally different from pitched roofing. Key considerations include:

  • Drainage Design - Proper slope is critical to prevent ponding.
  • Waterproofing - Unlike pitched roofs that shed water, flat roofs must be completely waterproof.
  • Material Selection - Different applications require different membrane systems.
  • Detail Work - Penetrations, edges, and transitions require expert attention.
